## NEGOTIATION FRAMEWORK (If Pattern A Emerges)

### Minimum Viable Partnership Terms

**Non-Negotiables:**
- Guaranteed minimum monthly payment OR revenue share with floor
- Monthly payment terms (NET-30 maximum, prefer NET-15)
- Editorial independence retained (you own IP, GCI gets license)
- Non-exclusive arrangement (you can publish independently and work with other partners)
- 30-day termination notice (either party can exit with minimal friction)

**Negotiable Elements:**
- Exact retainer amount (range: $3k-12k/month depending on scope)
- Revenue share percentage (range: 60/40 to 80/20 in your favor)
- Exclusivity within specific institutional segments (e.g., GCI exclusive for hedge fund distribution but you retain family office access)
- Equity component (you'd consider small GCI stake in LHM if strategic value clear)
- Data infrastructure support (vendor access, research tools)

### Partnership Structure Decision Tree

**IF: Retainer $8k+/month + editorial independence + data support**
→ **Priority partnership, delay other commitments**

**ELSE IF: Retainer $5-8k/month + editorial independence**
→ **Accept partnership, maintain parallel consulting/Substack development**

**ELSE IF: Retainer $3-5k/month + non-exclusive terms**
→ **Accept but treat as supplemental income, not primary focus**

**ELSE IF: Revenue share only, no guaranteed minimum**
→ **Decline unless GCI demonstrates $10k+/month revenue in first 90 days**

**ELSE IF: Retainer <$3k/month**
→ **Politely decline, opportunity cost too high**

**ELSE IF: Requires exclusivity without premium compensation**
→ **Hard decline, non-starter**

### Red Flags That Kill Deal

❌ **Equity ask without cash:** "We'd love to take 20% equity in exchange for distribution"  
→ Distribution is unproven, equity is real. Decline.

❌ **Speculative backend compensation:** "Revenue share kicks in after we hit $X in sales"  
→ If no guaranteed minimum, you're providing free content hoping for backend. Decline.

❌ **Broad exclusivity:** "You can't publish macro research anywhere else"  
→ Kills your independent Substack and consulting. Decline.

❌ **Extended payment terms:** "We pay quarterly in arrears"  
→ You're financing their cash flow. Counter with monthly NET-15 or decline.

❌ **Vague scope creep:** "We'd want you available for client calls/events as needed"  
→ Unlimited scope destroys capacity for other work. Define deliverables explicitly or decline.
