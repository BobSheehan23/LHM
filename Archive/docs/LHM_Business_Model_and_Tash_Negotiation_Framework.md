## I. Lighthouse Macro Operating Model

### Publication Cadence & Time Allocation

**Weekly Content Structure**:

1. **The Beacon** (Sunday)
   - Format: Long-form macro synthesis and tactical roadmap
   - Length: 2,000-5,000 words (varies by topic complexity)
   - Time Investment: 6-10 hours (research, writing, editing)
   - Positioning: Flagship analytical piece, primary value demonstration

2. **The Beam** (Tuesday & Thursday)
   - Format: Shorter tactical pieces on data, flow, and positioning
   - Length: ~500 words each
   - Time Investment: 90-120 minutes per piece (3-4 hours weekly total)
   - Positioning: Real-time market observation, timely tactical calls

3. **The Chartbook** (Friday)
   - Format: Visual overview of key macro and liquidity dynamics
   - Scope: 50-100 charts including proprietary indicators, dashboards, asset class positioning, market-wide technicals, individual securities
   - Time Investment: 4-6 hours (leveraging existing chart infrastructure)
   - Positioning: Visual storytelling, empirical rhythm + cross-asset diagnostics

4. **The Horizon** (First Monday of each month)
   - Format: Deep thematic exploration of structural forces
   - Length: 5,000-10,000 words (30-page bank-quality research pieces)
   - Time Investment: 15-25 hours monthly (4-6 hours weekly amortized)
   - Positioning: Prestige content, demonstrates comprehensive research capability

**Total Weekly Time Commitment**: 17-26 hours
- Sustainable for solo operation with proper systems
- Research compounds across pieces (charts built for Chartbook appear in Beacon/Horizon; frameworks developed in Beacon inform Beam tactical calls)

### Revenue Model

**Current Pricing Structure**:
- Monthly: $25
- Annual: $300 (16% discount)
- Positioning: Premium institutional newsletter tier

**Pricing Validation Against Comparables**:
- **Conks' Plumbing**: $300/year (Fed plumbing and repo mechanics)
- **Capital Flows**: $250/year (Cross-border flow analysis)
- **MacroTourist**: $420/year ($35/month) (Veteran trader with 25+ year track record)
- **The Macro Compass**: $199/year (Retail-focused educational, 152K subscribers)
- **Luke Gromen**: ~$120/year (Weekly-only cadence)

**Justification for $300/year Premium Tier**:
1. Credential stack: CFA + CMT (technical rigor signal)
2. Publication frequency: 4x weekly + monthly (16-20 pieces/month vs. 4-8 for most competitors)
3. Cross-asset scope: Full macro spectrum vs. narrower focus competitors
4. Chartbook production value: 50-100 charts weekly with proprietary indicators
5. Institutional accessibility: Bridges hedge fund quality with retail access

**Free vs. Paid Content Strategy**:
- **Free**: Sunday Beacon (audience builder, trust developer, publishing habit)
- **Paid**: Tuesday/Thursday Beam (tactical), Friday Chartbook (proprietary visuals), Monthly Horizon (deep-dives)
- Logic: Free content demonstrates analytical depth; paid content offers higher frequency and actionability

**Realistic Conversion Expectations**:
- Industry standard: 2-5% free-to-paid conversion (not Substack's marketed 10%)
- Strategy: Build 5,000-10,000 free subscribers before expecting meaningful paid conversions
- At 5,000 free subscribers with 3% conversion = 150 paid subscribers = $45K annual revenue
- At 10,000 free subscribers with 4% conversion = 400 paid subscribers = $120K annual revenue

### Production Infrastructure

**Current State**:
- Solo operation
- Python-based data workflows (reproducible, code-first)
- Existing chart infrastructure (templates, data pipelines)
- No hired support yet

**Scaling Triggers**:
- **200-300 paid subscribers** ($60-90K annual revenue): Hire part-time research analyst ($500-1,000/month, 10-15 hours weekly) for data compilation, initial chart drafts, administrative tasks
- **400-500 paid subscribers** ($120-150K annual revenue): Hire full-time analyst or editor for comprehensive production support

**Key Systems Needed**:
- 4-6 week content calendar buffer
- Template systems for recurring segments (weekly statistics, portfolio updates, data tables)
- Strict time-boxing (if newsletter production exceeds 15 hours weekly, reduce frequency)
- Monthly review: time per piece, engagement metrics, personal energy levels

4. **Marketing Assets**: Public track record and credible analysis = AIP legitimacy with institutional clients
5. **Credential Stack**: CFA + CMT + institutional background = trust signals for prospective AIP clients

**The Value Proposition**:

> "I'm not just executing allocation decisions based on existing frameworks. I'm building the research infrastructure that makes AIP institutionally credible and systematically rigorous. Without this, AIP is another relationship-based allocator. With this, AIP is a data-driven intelligence platform."

### Compensation Structure Options

**Option A: Pure Equity Play** (Current Structure - Needs Validation)
- $200K equity with meaningful ownership percentage (target: 10-25% depending on AIP valuation)
- Founder-equivalent terms (not employee options)
- Anti-dilution protection if AIP raises capital
- 4-year vest with 1-year cliff (standard)
- No cash salary, but expense reimbursement for research tools

**When This Works**:
- AIP has clear path to meaningful valuation ($5M+ in 3-5 years)
- Bob has sufficient runway from LHM revenue to sustain without salary
- Tash/GCI have strong fundraising capability and growth plan
- Bob is betting on upside rather than current income

**Option B: Hybrid Equity + Cash**
- $100-150K equity (still meaningful ownership stake)
- $50-75K annual base salary (covers baseline expenses, reduces LHM revenue pressure)
- Performance bonuses tied to AIP AUM growth or client acquisition
- Expense reimbursement for research infrastructure

**When This Works**:
- Bob needs cash flow stability while building LHM subscriber base
- De-risks the arrangement (salary covers downside, equity captures upside)
- AIP has current revenue or funding to support salary expense
- More sustainable if AIP growth takes longer than expected

**Option C: Revenue Share + Equity**
- $150-200K equity (maintain ownership stake)
- 10-20% revenue share on AIP management fees (participation in growth)
- Higher revenue share (25-30%) on clients sourced directly from LHM distribution
- No base salary, but strong incentive alignment

**When This Works**:
- AIP already has revenue or near-term client pipeline
- Bob wants direct participation in value creation beyond equity appreciation
- Aligns incentives around both client acquisition and client retention
- Rewards Bob for LHM distribution channel leverage

**Option D: Maximum Leverage Model** (Aggressive)
- $200K equity (founder-equivalent, anti-dilution protected)
- $75-100K base salary (stability while building LHM)
- 15% revenue share on AIP fees (participation in growth)
- 30% revenue share on LHM-sourced clients (distribution channel value)
- White-labeling agreement: AIP can use LHM research in client materials with attribution

**When This Works**:
- Bob has clear leverage (Tash needs the research infrastructure Bob provides)
- AIP has funding or revenue to support cash components
- Both parties view this as long-term partnership, not just employment
- Structure recognizes Bob is building the intellectual foundation of the business

### Red Flags to Watch For

**Signals That Structure Needs Revision**:

1. **Vague Equity Terms**: If Tash can't specify ownership percentage, vesting schedule, or dilution terms ‚Üí negotiate before committing
2. **Unrealistic Time Expectations**: If 3-5 hours weekly is temporary and Tash expects 20+ hours as AIP scales ‚Üí need cash compensation or reduced equity to reflect partial commitment
3. **IP Ownership Ambiguity**: If it's unclear who owns frameworks developed ‚Üí must clarify before building valuable IP
4. **LHM Conflict Expectations**: If Tash expects LHM subscribers to funnel to AIP or wants to control LHM content ‚Üí need clear boundaries
5. **Growth Without Resources**: If AIP expects rapid scaling without funding to hire support staff ‚Üí Bob's time commitment will explode

**Deal-Breaker Scenarios**:

- Equity represents <5% ownership with no anti-dilution protection
- Time commitment expected to grow significantly without corresponding compensation increase
- IP ownership reverts entirely to AIP/GCI (Bob can't use frameworks in LHM)
- LHM revenue/content subject to AIP approval or revenue sharing
- No clarity on exit terms or liquidation preference (equity could be worthless even if AIP succeeds)
