Collateral Fragility | 10-Chart Dashboard

This dashboard refocuses on indicators relevant to collateral tightness and funding conditions across U.S. money and Treasury markets. Each section describes the metric, why it matters, interpretation guidance, the latest reading, and cross-asset implications.

Data primarily from FRED/Federal Reserve and U.S. Treasury where available. For auction dynamics, dealer positions, and fails, please consult FRBNY/Treasury/SIFMA and reporting from WSJ, FT, Bloomberg, and Reuters.

Overnight Reverse Repo Facility Usage

What it is: Daily take-up at the Fed's Overnight Reverse Repo Facility (ON RRP).

Why we care: Elevated balances may indicate excess cash seeking safe collateral and can coincide with bill scarcity dynamics.

How to interpret: Rising usage can suggest collateral absorption by the Fed and a preference for secured placements. Declines may coincide with more collateral or higher private repo rates.

Latest value / interpretation: Latest reading on 2025-08-18 is 38.24. Levels and changes may be informative for collateral and funding conditions.

Cross-asset implications: Shifts can relate to changes in bill supply, TGA levels, and secured funding conditions.

Source: FRED H.4.1 (Federal Reserve) - RRPONTSYD. Additional context: WSJ/FT/Bloomberg/Reuters as noted in commentary.

Treasury General Account (TGA) Balance

What it is: The Treasury's cash balance at the Federal Reserve.

Why we care: Large changes in the TGA can withdraw or add reserves to the banking system, influencing collateral availability and repo conditions.

How to interpret: Rising TGA can drain reserves; falling TGA adds reserves. Quick swings can align with bill issuance and tax dates.

Latest value / interpretation: Latest reading on 2025-08-13 is 504.304. Levels and changes may be informative for collateral and funding conditions.

Cross-asset implications: Interactions with ON RRP and reserves can affect secured funding and liquidity premia.

Source: U.S. Treasury / FRED - WTREGEN. Additional context: WSJ/FT/Bloomberg/Reuters as noted in commentary.

SOFR vs Effective Fed Funds

What it is: Comparison of Secured Overnight Financing Rate and Effective Federal Funds Rate.

Why we care: Relative levels can reflect tightness in secured funding versus unsecured funding conditions.

How to interpret: SOFR trading above EFFR can indicate collateral-driven tightness; below can reflect ample collateral/liquidity.

Latest value / interpretation: Latest reading on 2025-07-01 is 4.44. Levels and changes may be informative for collateral and funding conditions.

Cross-asset implications: Secured funding levels may link to specials activity and Treasury collateral dynamics.

Source: FRED (Federal Reserve) - SOFR, FEDFUNDS. Additional context: WSJ/FT/Bloomberg/Reuters as noted in commentary.

SOFR minus Interest on Reserve Balances

What it is: Spread between the secured overnight funding rate and the Fed's administered IORB rate.

Why we care: A negative spread can indicate relative ease in secured markets; a positive spread may signal funding tightness or collateral scarcity.

How to interpret: Monitor deviations from typical ranges; sustained positives may indicate pressure in repo markets.

Latest value / interpretation: Latest reading on 2025-08-15 is -0.04. Levels and changes may be informative for collateral and funding conditions.

Cross-asset implications: Changes can be associated with ON RRP dynamics and collateral demand.

Source: FRED (Federal Reserve) - SOFR, IORB. Additional context: WSJ/FT/Bloomberg/Reuters as noted in commentary.

Bill Richness Proxy: 3M T-bill minus SOFR

What it is: Difference between 3-month Treasury bill yield and SOFR (monthly average).

Why we care: Bills trading rich to SOFR can be a sign of elevated collateral demand or scarcity.

How to interpret: Positive spreads suggest bills yielding more than SOFR; negative spreads may indicate rich bill pricing relative to secured funding.

Latest value / interpretation: Latest reading on 2025-07-01 is -0.0868. Levels and changes may be informative for collateral and funding conditions.

Cross-asset implications: Linked to ON RRP usage, TGA swings, and issuance mix.

Source: FRED - TB3MS, SOFR. Additional context: WSJ/FT/Bloomberg/Reuters as noted in commentary.

Yield Curve: 10Y minus 3M Treasury

What it is: Term structure slope between 10-year and 3-month Treasury rates.

Why we care: Curve shape can influence collateral preferences and hedge costs; inversions often pair with tighter funding conditions.

How to interpret: Inversions may coincide with higher demand for on-the-run collateral and richer bills.

Latest value / interpretation: Latest reading on 2025-07-01 is 0.1418. Levels and changes may be informative for collateral and funding conditions.

Cross-asset implications: Interacts with repo demand, futures basis, and liquidity premia.

Source: FRED - DGS10, TB3MS. Additional context: WSJ/FT/Bloomberg/Reuters as noted in commentary.

Federal Reserve Balance Sheet (Total Assets)

What it is: Total assets on the Federal Reserve's balance sheet.

Why we care: QE/QT alters the supply of reserves and collateral dynamics, potentially affecting repo conditions.

How to interpret: Balance sheet declines (QT) can coincide with lower reserves and tighter funding in some regimes.

Latest value / interpretation: Latest reading on 2025-08-13 is 6643615.0. Levels and changes may be informative for collateral and funding conditions.

Cross-asset implications: Relates to ON RRP usage, TGA moves, and Treasury issuance absorption.

Source: FRED H.4.1 - WALCL. Additional context: WSJ/FT/Bloomberg/Reuters as noted in commentary.

Reserve Balances with Federal Reserve Banks

What it is: Aggregate reserve balances held by depository institutions at the Fed.

Why we care: Reserves levels influence money market conditions and availability of balance sheet to intermediate collateral.

How to interpret: Falling reserves may align with tighter conditions; rising reserves with easing.

Latest value / interpretation: Latest reading on 2025-08-13 is 3320.05. Levels and changes may be informative for collateral and funding conditions.

Cross-asset implications: Connects to TGA, ON RRP, and repo spreads.

Source: FRED H.4.1 - WRESBAL. Additional context: WSJ/FT/Bloomberg/Reuters as noted in commentary.
