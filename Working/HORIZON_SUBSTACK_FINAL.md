## The Inflation Regime: The Last Mile Problem

Core PCE sits at 2.8%â€”80 basis points above target despite 18 months of restrictive policy. The goods-services bifurcation explains the stickiness.

**[CHART 8: Core PCE Components â€” Goods vs Services Bifurcation]**
*Dual series: Goods inflation (Ocean Blue #0089D1) vs Services/Supercore inflation (Dusk Orange #FF6723). Horizontal line at 2.0% target. Current readings annotated.*

**Goods Disinflation: Mission Accomplished**

Durable goods prices have declined 3.2% year-over-year. Supply chains normalized. China export deflation continues (PPI negative for 23 consecutive months). The Bloomberg Commodity Index is down 18% from 2022 peaks. The goods disinflation phase is complete.

**Services Inflation: The Persistent Challenge**

Services inflation runs at 3.8%â€”the "supercore" component that excludes both goods and housing. This is the Fed's true concern:

- Medical care services: +4.2% YoY
- Auto insurance: +13.8% YoY (largest contributor)
- Food services: +4.1% YoY
- Financial services: +5.2% YoY

Three structural factors prevent services disinflation: wage cost pass-through (service sector compensation up 4.6%), housing cost lagged effects (market rents moderate but CPI shelter still elevated), and insurance cost dynamics (higher rates increase insurance company costs, passed to consumers).

**The Implicit Trade-Off**

The Fed cut rates in December despite core PCE running 80 bps above target. This represents a regime shift: accept 2.5-2.8% inflation to preserve labor market stability and prevent financial system stress.

The calculation appears to be: further rate hikes damage the labor market while producing limited incremental services disinflation (since services inflation reflects structural wage dynamics rather than aggregate demand). Combined with massive fiscal deficits and constrained dealer balance sheets, the Fed chose plumbing stability over inflation perfection.

Our projection: inflation stalls at 2.5-2.8% through 2026, becoming the de facto "new normal" unless wage growth decelerates sharply or productivity accelerates materially.

But ask yourself: why would the Fed need $40 billion monthly in "reserve management" when reserves sit at $3.3 trillionâ€”well above any historical measure of "ample"?

The answer: it's not about the level of reserves. It's about their distribution and the plumbing constraints preventing smooth circulation.

## The Positioning Framework

The three-pillar analysis converges on a coherent regime diagnosis: Q1 liquidity support creates tactical opportunity, Q2-Q4 fundamental constraints force repricing.

**[CHART 14: Asset Allocation Roadmap â€” Q1 vs Q2-Q4]**
*Two-panel bar chart or allocation wheel showing Q1 positioning (45% equity, underweight duration, 5-7% gold, 1-2% BTC) vs Q2-Q4 defensive shift (35-40% equity, 15-20% cash). Color-coded by asset class.*

The implementation is straightforward:

**Q1 2026: Capture the Liquidity**

Maintain equity exposure but tilt toward quality. The RMP tailwind will lift all boats initially, but concentration in quality names (strong balance sheets, high free cash flow, secular growth drivers) provides downside protection when the tide turns.

- 45% equity exposure, tilted to quality and semiconductors
- Underweight long-duration Treasuries (the term premium repricing is coming)
- 5-7% gold allocation as fiscal hedge (benefits from either inflationary melt-up or crisis flight-to-safety)
- 1-2% Bitcoin as liquidity proxy (correlates with RMP pace)

The semiconductor allocation reflects AI capex momentum, which provides partial insulation from cyclical concerns. Nvidia, TSMC, and the broader AI infrastructure buildout have structural demand drivers that persist regardless of macro conditions.

**Q2-Q4 2026: Prepare for Repricing**

As Q1 rally matures, reduce beta systematically. The RMP tailwind fades by mid-year. Fiscal issuance pressure intensifies during Q2 refunding season. Dealer balance sheet constraints bind tighter.

- Reduce equity beta to 35-40%
- Raise cash to 15-20% for opportunistic deployment
- Maintain credit quality (avoid HY, prefer IG)
- Extend duration selectively if recession signals emerge

The key is maintaining optionality. Cash becomes a position when volatility is cheap and liquidity is at risk. The 15-20% cash allocation provides deployment capacity for either scenario: buying the plumbing accident dip or rotating to duration if recession confirms.

**Hedging Strategy:**

- Long VIX calls (3-month tenor, 30-35 strike): Provides convex exposure to plumbing accident scenario. Current VIX at ~17 makes call spreads relatively cheap.
- Gold as dual-purpose hedge: Benefits from inflationary concerns (base case) and crisis flight-to-safety (stress scenarios). The fiscal doom loop supports gold structurally.
- 2Y Treasuries for recession protection: Positive carry while waiting. If recession triggers, 2Y rallies as Fed cuts aggressively.
