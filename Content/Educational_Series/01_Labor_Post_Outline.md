## SECTION 1: THE FATAL FLAW OF HEADLINE DATA (400-500 words)

### Why the Unemployment Rate Is Lagging

- **The unemployment rate tells you where you've been, not where you're going**
- It's a stock (level), not a flow (rate of change)
- Historical evidence: unemployment rate bottomed in May 2007 at 4.4%, recession started December 2007
- By the time unemployment rises meaningfully, the recession is already 6-9 months old

### The Visibility Problem

- Media covers the headline because it's simple
- But simplicity comes at the cost of signal
- "Watching the unemployment rate is like checking your rear-view mirror to drive forward"

### What We're Looking For

- Leading indicators, not lagging ones
- Flows, not stocks
- Worker behavior, not employer announcements

## SECTION 2: FLOWS VS. STOCKS - THE CORE DISTINCTION (500-600 words)

### The Taxonomy of Labor Data

**Stocks (Lagging):**
- Unemployment rate
- Payrolls level
- Employment-population ratio

**Flows (Leading):**
- Quits rate (workers leaving voluntarily)
- Hires rate (employers actually bringing people on)
- Layoffs rate (employers cutting)
- Temp help employment (first hired, first fired)

### The Quits Rate: The Economy's Truth Serum

- Workers quit when they're confident they can find something better
- When quits collapse, workers are seeing something employers haven't admitted yet
- "The quits rate doesn't lie. Workers vote with their feet, and the ballots are already counted."

**Historical Track Record:**
- Quits rate below 2.0% has preceded every recession since 1990
- Lead time: 6-9 months
- Current reading: 1.9%

## SECTION 3: BENEATH THE HEADLINES - WHY SEGMENTATION MATTERS (600-700 words)

### The Average Obscures the Distribution

"The average American has 1.9 children and 0.7 dogs. Have you ever met this person?"

Headlines give you the average. But averages hide the distribution. In labor markets, the distribution is where the signal lives.

### The Segmentation Hierarchy

Different groups show stress at different times:

**Leading (First to crack):**
- Youth (16-24)
- Less than high school education
- Black and Hispanic workers
- Temp help services

**Lagging (Last to show stress):**
- Prime-age (25-54)
- Bachelor's degree+
- White workers
- Healthcare employment

### Case Study 1: Youth Unemployment as Canary

Walk through the 2007-2009 example:
- Youth unemployment crossed 11% in early 2008
- Overall unemployment still at 5%
- Youth led by 3-4 months and showed 2x amplitude
- **Lesson:** If you were watching youth, you saw the recession coming

### Case Study 2: The Job-Hopper Premium Collapse

Walk through the current cycle:
- 2021-2022: Job switchers earning 5-6 ppts more than stayers
- Workers quitting aggressively, employers competing fiercely
- Jan 2026: Premium collapsed to 0.2 ppts
- Translation: "The grass is no longer greener. Workers know it."

### Case Study 3: Geographic Diffusion

- National data can be dragged up by a few strong states
- Track: % of states with rising unemployment (3-month)
- Currently: 58% of states showing rising unemployment
- **Lesson:** Weakness is spreading, not isolated
